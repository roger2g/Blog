WALKING IN NATURE HAS MANY BENEFITS FOR THE BRAIN

LACK OF NATURE AND DISEASE

During the history of human evolution, our species has always been around nature. for this reason, illnesses such as depression were infrequent, as were anxiety and other problems with concentration and lack of focus. currently, much of our life is concentrated in large cities, which can harm mental health, especially in the worsening of depressive conditions.
for this reason, researchers are investigating how walking in nature can benefit the brain, with the aim of making our lives calmer and happier.

KNOW SOME BENEFITS AND HOW YOU CAN ENJOY IT EVEN IF YOU LIVE IN BIG CITIES

In the study conducted with 63 adults, the scientists were able to check the MRI scan for the difference between walks in the forest, in the countryside, and walks made in urban areas surrounded by nature, such as parks. Among the advantages positive results observed are the reduction of blood pressure, control of anxiety and various improvements, such as mood, focus, sleep. Concentration and memory are also not left out and benefit from nature walks.
This is because, according to the researchers, the cerebral amygdala, a structure in the brain responsible for processing stress, has reduced activity. Scientists even observed an interesting effect on the brain of urban walkers. Brain amygdala activity did not worsen, even in a busy environment.
So, this means that even if you cannot walk in the woods or parks outside the city, your body will not suffer from additional stress. For this reason, to reduce stress, whether in more distant locations or not, the important is to put yourself in motion and be in touch with the nature.
