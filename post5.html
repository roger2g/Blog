THE IMPORTANCE OF VOLCANOES FOR LIFE ON EARTH

WHAT IS A VOLCANO?

Volcanoes are openings in the Earth's crust through which magma from the mantle is able to flow to the external environment. It may seem that volcanic eruptions serve no purpose other than to destroy everything around them. However, volcanoes bring many benefits to life on Earth. Without the eruptions, communities of farmers wouldn't be able to grow food, certain building materials wouldn't be available, and the atmosphere wouldn't be rich in oxygen.

DEGASSING

Billions of years ago, Earth's atmosphere was composed mostly of silicate vapour. During early volcanic eruptions, carbon dioxide and water vapor were among the gases released into the atmosphere. The release of these gases helped to form the planet's aerobic atmosphere.

FERTILE LAND

The most fertile land in the world can be found at the base of a volcano. Volcanic rocks are full of minerals and, when broken down, provide valuable nutrients for plants. Farmers plant and harvest in the volcanic valleys.

CONSTRUCTION MATERIALS

Once magma, also known as lava, reaches the surface, it cools quickly. After complete cooling, the magma solidifies into black stones, which are used in soaps and abrasive cleaning products. Dry lava is also used to build roads and buildings.

NEW LANDS

The Hawaiian Islands were created by volcanic eruptions millions of years ago. These submerged volcanoes have erupted numerous times, leading to a buildup of magma that is pushed towards the surface. There are volcanoes on the Pacific tectonic plate, located below thermal ocean waters and moving 8 cm per year. The movement of the Pacific plate is important for the continued formation of islands.
