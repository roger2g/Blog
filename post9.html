HOW DOES THE MOON AFFECT LIFE ON EARTH?

MOON PHASES

The phases of the Moon are the variations of the Moon's position in relation to the Earth and the Sun , that is, the cycles in which it is. In total, there are four phases of the Moon: full, waning, new and crescent. Each of the phases lasts seven to eight days and is observable in different ways here on Earth, depending on which hemisphere you are in. In addition, the Moon has three main movements:

  • Rotation, which is the movement of rotating around its own axis;
  • Revolution, which is the rotation around the Earth;
  • Translation, which is the movement around the Sun, rotating along with the Earth.

It is worth noting, the position and shape that the Moon acquires in our eyes, does not vary by the light of the Moon itself. The satellite does not produce its own light, so the Moon's luminosity is provided by the Sun.

HOW DOES THE MOON AFFECT LIFE ON EARTH?

Due to the Moon's gravity , it exerts a direct influence on Earth. The satellite's gravitational force is capable of moving large masses of water, according to the change in its phases . This causes the high or low tides of the oceans.

As for agriculture, the influence of the Moon on plants has been known to farmers since ancient times. For example, for millennia, in China, the knowledge about cutting bamboo, which must be carried out between the waning and the new moon, which is when the sap and humidity inside the trunks is lower.

For many animals, especially birds, the Moon is essential for migration and navigation. For others, their reproduction coincides with the specific phases of the lunar cycle. In the case of predators, for example, the full moon is in the way, as it brings more light at night, making hunting difficult.

THE INFLUENCE OF THE MOON ON THE HUMAN BODY

Contrary to what many beliefs say, the Moon has no direct influence on the human body . There is no scientific evidence to suggest that cutting hair at a certain stage makes it grow faster or slower.

The woman's menstrual cycle also shows no correlation with the satellite. At least so far, no scientific study has been able to prove any relationship between menstruation and the Moon. Also, the full cycle of the Moon, from one new moon to another, lasts about 29 days, about a day longer than the menstrual cycle. There is also no evidence that the Moon influences the birth of more boys or girls.
