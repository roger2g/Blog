WHY DOES THE EARTH NEED THE SUN?

DID YOU KNOW THAT THE ENERGY OF THE SUN IS ESSENTIAL FOR LIFE ON EARTH? UNDERSTAND THIS STORY BETTER

If the Sun did not exist or would cease to exist, the Earth's orbit would be modified. The path that our planet takes around the Sun is a consequence of the gravity between the two bodies. Without the star, this path would be changed. Earth could have gravitational interaction with another object, for example.

It is important to remember that without the Sun, the Earth would not have formed. Our Solar System formed about 4.6 billion years ago from the same cloud of gas and dust. From most of this material, the Sun emerged. What was left gave rise to Earth, the other planets, comets and asteroids.

The energy of the Sun is responsible for keeping the planet warm, for the movement of the oceans, for the formation of winds (heating the air) and for the water cycle. Without the energy of the Sun, we would live without light and the seasons would not exist. Our planet would be very cold and atmosphere would be denser. There would be little wind and, little by little, the waters would freeze.

In addition to being essential for the Earth, the Sun is important for the beings that live here. Almost all of them depend on the light and heat of the Sun to survive. Plants need light to carry out photosynthesis , a process in which they transform carbon dioxide and water into glucose, proteins and vitamins, releasing oxygen into the atmosphere. Without sunlight, plants would not be able to feed and would die.

In addition to ensuring the survival of plants, photosynthesis is essential for animals and humans. Without plants, the entire food chain would be affected: herbivorous animals would have nothing to eat and, little by little, they would also die. Then carnivorous animals and humans would also run out of food.

Heat also favors the action of enzymes (substances that cause chemical reactions inside cells) present in plants and animals. Some animals, such as fish, amphibians and reptiles, can improve metabolic activity when the temperature of the environment is high. Thus, the organism gains energy to continue functioning.

The sun also plays an important role in our health. For strong bones and teeth, we need vitamin D, which is not readily available in most foods. What is found in them is a substance that turns into the vitamin when exposed to the sun's rays. A lack of vitamin D causes problems with your teeth and makes your bones weak.
